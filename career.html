International Career

While playing for Ajax FC, Zlatan made his debut for the Swedish National Team in 2001. On 7 October 2001, he played his first competitive match, a 2002 World Cup qualifier against Azerbaijan, scoring his first ever international goal in a 3–0 win to help Sweden qualify for their upcoming tournament.
After Zlatan started making a name for himself, he brought more fans not only to the National team, but to every club he played for. A few years later, with the help of Zlatan, Sweden qualified for Euro 2004 as group winner's, with Ibrahimović scoring three goals throughout the qualifying campaign. On 4 September 2014, Ibrahimović scored his 50th international goal against Estonia,making him the Swedish national team's all-time leading goalscorer.


Club Career

Throughout his career, Ibrahimović has played for 8 major soccer clubs, beginning at Malmo FC. By the end of his career at Malmo, he had made 47 appearances and scored 18 goals.
In 2009, Zlatan made a major desision by leaving Inter Milan and joining Barcelona FC, flabbergasting the entire world of soccer. In total, Zlatan scored a total of 22 goals with 46 appearances.
2012 was one of the biggest years for Zlatan when he moved from AC Milan, to Paris Saint-Germain. He spent a total of 4 years in the club, helping them top their leagues every season since.
Ibrahimović is never shy of controversial desisions, which brought him to move to his current club, Manchester United, where he has already scored 4 goals in only 5 appearances, bringing his career total to 396 goals and 681 appearances.
