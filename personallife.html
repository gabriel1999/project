Personal Life

Ibrahimović has two siblings and three half-siblings. He has two sons with his wife, Helena Seger, named Maximilian and Vincent. He currently lives outside Manchester, although he still has a home in Malmö where he spends his time of the field.
Among his trophies, Ibrahimović has also received an honorary black belt in taekwondo. Zlatan is fluent in five languages, Swedish, Bosnian, English, Spanish and Italian. The soccer star is both Muslim and Catholic, coming from his parents. Zlatan's confidence has helped him make a name for himself , literally, the name Zlatan was trademarked in May 2003 at the Swedish Patent and Registration Office.


Controversies

Ibrahimović has been involved in many violent situations with teammates. For example, former Ajax teammate Rafael van der Vaart publicly accused Ibrahimović of deliberately injuring him during the game. Ibrahimović responded by threatening to break both of Van der Vaart's legs.
Another incident is when Zlatan punched Ajax teammate, Mido, in the dressing room after the he had thrown a pair of scissors at him.
But despite the violence and arrogance, his fans have remained loyal to him and gained more love for the soccer star.
